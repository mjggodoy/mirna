Description
Over the last few years, many microRNA-based databases have been created and released to the public. Unfortunately, many of these databases have been created as isolated databases in which users have to navigate through them to obtain integrated information on microRNAs. Some examples of these databases are miREnvironment which contains information on environmental factors and phenotypes; SM2miR that stores information on small molecules and their effect on microRNA expression or MicroCosm targets and TarBase that provide information on predicted and experimentally validated microRNA targets. Therefore, the aim of iMIRNA is to provide a database that integrates information about microRNA expression and microRNA-target interactions, phenotypes, SNPs which affect to microRNA targets, environmental factors and their influence on microRNA expression etc. Currently, iMIRNA contains integrated information from databases such as HMDD, MicroCosm , mir2Disease , miRCancer , miRDB , mirDIP , miRdSNP , mirEnvironment , Phenomir , SM2miR and TarBase. We have also included information related to recent discovered microRNAs from miRPlant and VIRmiRNA that stores experimentally validated viral microRNAS and their targets. As miRBase is the main microRNA database, we have used the microRNA names and their accession numbers as standards.
