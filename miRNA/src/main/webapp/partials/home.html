Description
Over the last few years, many microRNA-based databases have been created and released to the public. Unfortunately, many of these databases have been created as isolated databases that users have to navigate through them to obtain integrated information on microRNAs. Some examples of these databases are a bridge for microRNAs, environmental factors and phenotypes such as miREnvironment and SM2miR and others, like MicroCosm targets or TarBase provide information on predicted and experimentally validated microRNA targets. The aim of iMIRNA is to provide a database that integrates information about microRNA expression and interactions, phenotypes, SNPs which affect to microRNA targets, environmental factors and their influence on microRNA expression etc. Currently, iMIRNA contains integrated information from databases such as HMDD, MicroCosm , mir2Disease , miRCancer , miRDB , mirDIP , miRdSNP , mirEnvironment , Phenomir and TarBase. We have also included information related to recent discovered microRNAs from miRPlant and MIRmiRNA that stores experimentally validated viral microRNAS and their targets. As miRBase is the main microRNA database, we have used the microRNA names and their accession numbers as standards.
